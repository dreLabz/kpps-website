Sport and performance psychology focuses on training the mental side of performance—how you think, feel, and execute in key moments. Whether in sport, school, work, or other high-pressure environments, psychological skills can be trained just like physical ones.

Sessions are designed to build core mental skills so you can perform consistently when it matters most. This work supports both peak performance and overall well-being, helping you show up prepared, grounded, and confident in your craft and in life.

Sessions are one-on-one, virtual, and completely confidential. Each session is built around your specific performance goals and adjusted as those goals evolve over time.

FAQ

Common
Questions

Do you take insurance?

Dr. Kraska operates as a private pay practice. Sessions are not billed directly to insurance. Depending on your plan, you may be eligible for out-of-network reimbursement. Contact your insurance provider before your first session to ask about your out-of-network mental health benefits.

What states can you see clients in?

Dr. Kraska is licensed in Georgia and holds PSYPACT authorization to practice telepsychology in 42 states. If you are unsure whether your state is included, reach out and she will confirm before scheduling.

What does a first session look like?

It starts with a free 15-minute consultation. A conversation about what you are working on, what you are hoping to accomplish, and whether working together is a good fit. From there, individual sessions are scheduled based on your goals and availability.

What is the difference between sport psychology and clinical psychology?

Sport psychology is about building the mental skills that help you perform under pressure such as confidence, focus, routines, and composure. Clinical psychology addresses what is happening beneath the surface such as anxiety, depression, trauma, and burnout.

Do you work with high school athletes?

Yes. Dr. Kraska works with high school adolescents and adults across all levels including recreational, high school, collegiate, and professional. All backgrounds and identities are warmly welcomed.
